John Harvard

John Harvard (1607–1638), an English minister, left a significant bequest to the Massachusetts Bay Colony's college, leading to the institution being named Harvard College. A graduate of Emmanuel College, University of Cambridge, he emigrated to New England in 1637. Harvard University regards him as a highly honored founder, commemorated with a prominent statue in Harvard Yard.

Biography

John Harvard was born in Southwark, London, and later immigrated to Massachusetts Bay Colony. He played a crucial role in the establishment of Harvard College.

Marriage and Emigration

John Harvard married Ann Sadler in 1636 and, shortly after, died of tuberculosis at the age of 31. His library and a bequest of £800 were left to "New College" in Cambridge.

Death

John Harvard succumbed to tuberculosis without heirs, leaving a substantial bequest of £800 and his library to "New College" in Cambridge. The institution, founded in 1636, changed its name to "Harvard College" in 1639, becoming the first referred to as a university in the Massachusetts Constitution of 1780.

Legacy and Bequest

John Harvard's bequest laid the foundation for Harvard College, now Harvard University, making him a key figure in American higher education history.

Memorials and Tributes

Numerous memorials and tributes exist in honor of John Harvard, symbolizing his enduring legacy in education.
